A collection of expository articles, seminar slides, and final course projects. Unlike research papers, the goal of these documents is pedagogical: to introduce specific topics, survey existing literature, or present classical proofs in a more accessible language.

Introduction to Holomorphic Dynamics

Final Project • Complex Analysis II (2023)

An expository paper exploring the basic properties of the Julia and Fatou sets for rational maps. We discuss the classification of fixed points, the dynamics of $z \mapsto z^2+c$, and provide visual examples of the Mandelbrot set generated using Python.

The Circle Method: A Heuristic Approach

Junior Seminar Talk • UNAM (2022)

Slides from a talk aimed at undergraduate students. The presentation focuses on the intuition behind the "Major" and "Minor" arcs in the Hardy-Littlewood method, avoiding heavy estimates to highlight the geometric interpretation of the integral.

Generating Functions in Combinatorics

Discrete Mathematics • (2021)

A short survey on the use of ordinary and exponential generating functions to solve recurrence relations. Includes the derivation of the Catalan numbers and applications to partition theory.
